§ 01 — The trick

One source.
Two finished cuts.

CuePair captures a wide 16:9 frame, then renders a portrait crop that follows your face in real time. You don't choose between vertical and horizontal — you ship both, from the same take, in the time it takes to make a coffee.
A. Renders happen in parallel after you stop recording — typically faster than realtime on Apple silicon.
B. Crop center is smoothed; small head movements don't jitter the frame.
C. Outputs land side-by-side in a single folder, ready to drop into your scheduler.
§ 02 — Teleprompter

Reads you.
Not the other way around.

Speech recognition runs on-device and the script scrolls to match your cadence. Slow down to think, the prompter waits. Pop it out to a second monitor when you want to look down the lens, not down at your keyboard.

A.

Voice-paced scrolling

On-device speech recognition matches the script to your cadence. Pause to think and the line waits for you. No knobs, no calibration.

B.

Pop out to a second display

Send the prompter to an external monitor positioned right under the lens. Get eye contact back. Your camera doesn't care that you're reading.

C.

Adjustable column width

Narrow the reading column to keep your eyes still. Wide for prose, tight for tight cuts. The geometry of the page changes with you.

§ 04 — Scope

What it
doesn't do.

A short list, on purpose. CuePair does one workflow, well. The things it doesn't do, it doesn't do.

  • 01 No filters or beauty effects. You look like you.
  • 02 No timeline editor. Cut in Final Cut.
  • 03 No SDKs, plugins, extensions. It's a Mac app.
  • 04 No cloud sync, no accounts. Files live in Movies/.
  • 05 No telemetry. None. Not "anonymized" — absent.
  • 06 No AI captions, summaries, or rewriting. Just your words.
